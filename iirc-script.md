# 5-Minute Speaking Script
## Campus Navigation Application Using Augmented Reality

**Total target time:** 5:00 minutes | **Pace:** ~130–140 words per minute | **Word count:** ~670 words

---

### SLIDE 1 — TITLE *(15 seconds)*

Good morning/afternoon, everyone. I'm [your name], and together with my co-researchers from St. Paul University Philippines, we are pleased to present our study titled **"Campus Navigation Application Using Augmented Reality."**

---

### SLIDE 2 — INTRODUCTION *(40 seconds)*

How many of you have walked into a new building and felt completely lost? Now imagine that's your first day at university.

Augmented Reality has rapidly evolved from industrial use into mainstream technology, reshaping education, healthcare, and daily life. Yet despite this progress, large university campuses — including ours at SPUP — still rely on static maps and signage that often confuse new students, visitors, and even staff.

This study addresses that gap by exploring how AR and AI can transform campus wayfinding into a smarter, more interactive experience.

---

### SLIDE 3 — RESEARCH QUESTIONS *(25 seconds)*

Our study sought to answer three questions:

First, what challenges do visitors face when navigating the campus?
Second, what AI-based application can be developed to address these challenges?
And third, what enhancements can further improve the system?

---

### SLIDE 4 — METHODS *(45 seconds)*

We used a **descriptive-developmental research design**.

Our participants included students, faculty, and visitors who shared their navigation challenges, as well as IT specialists who evaluated the final system.

We used an interview guide to identify the problems, and the **ISO 25010 standardized questionnaire** to assess software quality.

The data was analyzed through thematic analysis for the qualitative responses, and weighted mean with a Likert scale for the quantitative evaluation — ensuring both the user perspective and the technical perspective were captured.

---

### SLIDE 5 — KEY FINDINGS, PART 1 *(50 seconds)*

For the first research question, our interviews revealed that visitors, new students, and even staff commonly experience **confusion when locating buildings and offices**, **lost time** during their first visits, and a heavy **reliance on static maps and word-of-mouth directions** that are often inaccurate. These challenges are made worse by the campus's **large and constantly evolving topography** — with unfinished facilities and frequently changing room assignments.

To answer the second question, we developed the **SPUP AR Navigation System** — a mobile application that loads campus maps and AR assets behind a branded opening screen, then guides users through a camera-based interface. Users simply select a destination — like the Registrar, Cashier, or Information Office — and an augmented reality arrow appears on screen, leading them straight to the location. The app also recognizes landmarks and overlays informative AR text in real time.

---

### SLIDE 6 — KEY FINDINGS, PART 2 *(40 seconds)*

When evaluated using ISO 25010 standards, the system complied **to a great extent** — scoring high in functionality, usability, efficiency, reliability, and compatibility.

For our third research question, IT experts recommended expanding AR coverage to the entire university, adding online features, allowing users to set their own custom locations, and refining the UI/UX for greater ease of use.

---

### SLIDE 7 — CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS *(45 seconds)*

In conclusion, navigation difficulties at SPUP are real — visitors experience confusion, lost time, and over-reliance on static maps that traditional signage cannot fully resolve.

Our study shows that an AR-based mobile application is a viable solution: the developed SPUP AR Navigation System met ISO 25010 software quality standards **to a great extent**, validating its functionality, usability, and reliability. And while effective, the system can still be strengthened through broader coverage, online features, and refined UI/UX.

We therefore recommend that the University's ICT Department pilot and roll out the system across all campus zones, and that it be integrated into freshman orientation and campus-tour programs. Future researchers may also explore Bluetooth beacons, Ultra-Wideband positioning, and machine learning for dynamic, multi-floor navigation.

---

### SLIDE 8 — IMPLICATIONS *(40 seconds)*

Practically, this system reduces confusion and saves time for everyone navigating our campus.

Institutionally, it positions SPUP as a model for **smart-campus initiatives** in Philippine higher education, and supports our digital transformation goals.

Theoretically, it demonstrates how AR and AI can be meaningfully integrated for user-centered navigation — and it directly contributes to **Sustainable Development Goals 4, 9, and 11**: Quality Education, Innovation, and Sustainable Cities.

---

### SLIDE 9 — REFERENCES *(10 seconds)*

These are the five key references that grounded our study, ranging from AR-based indoor navigation systems to comparative studies on spatial knowledge acquisition.

---

### SLIDE 10 — THANK YOU *(10 seconds)*

Thank you for your time and attention. We would now be glad to answer any questions you may have.

## DELIVERY TIPS

- **Practice with a timer.** Aim for 4:45 in rehearsal to leave room for natural pauses.
- **Slow down on numbers and key terms** — "ISO 25010," "to a great extent," "Sustainable Development Goals 4, 9, and 11."
- **Pause briefly** after the opening question (Slide 2) and after stating "to a great extent" (Slide 6) — these are emphasis moments.
- **Don't rush the conclusion.** This is what judges remember most.
- **Smile on the Thank You slide.** First impressions matter; last impressions linger.
